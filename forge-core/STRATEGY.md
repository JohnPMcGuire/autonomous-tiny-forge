# Forge Core strategy

## Mission

Build a bounded autonomous venture studio that compounds evidence and reusable capabilities while pursuing profitable digital businesses.

## Current strategic thesis

The best first company is likely Search Intelligence: a service and eventual product that audits ecommerce search, product listing pages, catalog quality, facets, redirects, recommendations, and conversion opportunities.

Reasons:

- Strong founder-domain fit.
- Business customers already pay for ecommerce optimization.
- A concierge audit can reach revenue before a full platform exists.
- Repeated audits can create proprietary benchmark and issue-pattern data.
- The work can later support a real ecommerce test property without making merchandise the primary business.

This thesis remains unvalidated until external behavior demonstrates willingness to pay.

## Phase roadmap

### Phase 0: Core

Create governance, opportunity and experiment ledgers, deterministic validation, decision records, and repeatable handoffs.

Exit criteria:

- Every run can identify the current strategy and next action from repository state.
- Direction changes require evidence and a recorded decision.
- Spending and customer-impacting actions are blocked by default.
- Core state passes deterministic validation.

### Phase 1: Revenue validation

Sell a manually reviewed ecommerce search or site analysis before building broad automation.

Exit criteria:

- At least one unrelated external customer pays.
- Delivery steps and customer-valued findings are recorded.
- A repeatable acquisition path has at least one credible signal.

### Phase 2: Productize repeated work

Automate only the checks and delivery steps proven useful during paid audits.

### Phase 3: Recurring company

Reach recurring revenue with measurable retention and positive contribution margin.

### Phase 4: Portfolio

Consider a second company only after the first has repeatable revenue or the evidence supports an intentional pivot.

## Non-goals during Phase 0

- Launching multiple companies.
- Purchasing ads.
- Granting autonomous financial authority.
- Collecting sensitive customer data.
- Building a large platform before a paid validation experiment.
- Treating commits, features, or app count as business success.

## Strategy change protocol

A direction change requires all of the following:

1. A named prior decision or thesis being changed.
2. New evidence unavailable or unconsidered when the prior decision was made.
3. At least three alternatives compared using the current scoring model.
4. Expected benefits, risks, opportunity costs, and reversibility documented.
5. A new decision entry with a review date.

A change in implementation that does not alter customer, problem, business model, capital allocation, or portfolio priority is not a strategy change.

## North-star question

What is the highest expected-value use of the next bounded unit of work and capital, given the evidence currently available?
